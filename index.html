Why Forests Matter

Discover the critical role forests play in sustaining life on Earth

Oxygen Production

Forests produce over 28% of the world's oxygen, purifying the air we breathe every day. A single mature tree can provide enough oxygen for two people per year.

Wildlife Habitat

Forests are home to 80% of terrestrial biodiversity, providing shelter, food, and protection for millions of species, from tiny insects to large mammals.

Climate Regulation

Forests act as carbon sinks, absorbing CO2 and helping regulate global temperatures. They store about 296 gigatons of carbon worldwide.

Water Cycle

Forests play a crucial role in maintaining water cycles, preventing soil erosion, and ensuring clean water sources for communities worldwide.

Livelihoods

Over 1.6 billion people depend on forests for their livelihoods, including food, medicine, fuel, and economic opportunities.

Human Health

Forest environments reduce stress, improve mental health, and provide natural medicines that have been used for thousands of years.

Major Threats to Forests

Understanding the challenges we face in forest conservation

  • Deforestation: Clearing forests for agriculture, urbanization, and industrial purposes
  • Forest Fires: Increasing frequency and intensity due to climate change
  • Pests & Diseases: Outbreaks that can devastate entire forest ecosystems
  • Climate Change: Altered weather patterns affecting forest health
  • Illegal Logging: Unsustainable timber harvesting practices
  • Urban Expansion: Growing cities encroaching on forest areas
